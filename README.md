## ğŸ¯ Business Questions & Analysis

### Q1. Top Revenue-Generating Customers
Identifies customers who generate the highest total revenue.

**Business value:**  
Supports retention strategies, loyalty programs, and targeted marketing efforts.

---

### Q2. Customers with No Orders
Finds registered customers who have never placed an order.

**Business value:**  
Highlights inactive users and potential onboarding or engagement issues.

---

### Q3. One-Time Customers
Identifies customers who have placed exactly one order.

**Business value:**  
Helps detect high churn-risk customers and design follow-up campaigns.

---

### Q4. Most Active Customers
Ranks customers by the number of orders placed.

**Business value:**  
Enables identification of loyal customers for VIP or reward programs.

---

### Q5. Average Order Value per Customer
Calculates the average monetary value of orders placed by each customer.

**Business value:**  
Provides insight into purchasing behavior and supports customer segmentation.

---

### Q6. Inactive Customers (Last 6 Months)
Identifies customers who have not placed any orders in the last six months
or have never ordered.

**Business value:**  
Supports churn analysis and reactivation strategies.

---

### Q7. Revenue Concentration (Top Customers)
Measures how much of the total revenue is generated by the top customers (Top 5).

**Business value:**  
Reveals dependency on a small customer group and highlights revenue risk.

---

### Q8. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
Calculates the total revenue generated by each customer over their lifetime.

**Business value:**  
Helps prioritize long-term retention and high-value customer acquisition.

---

### Q9. New vs Returning Customers
Classifies customers as **New** (one order) or **Returning** (multiple orders)
and aggregates each group.

**Business value:**  
Provides insight into whether growth is driven by acquisition or repeat purchases.
